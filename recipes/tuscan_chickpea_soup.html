Tuscan Chickpea Soup

No description

Yield

0 Servings

Ingredients

  • 3/4 teaspoon sea salt
  • 6 tablespoons grated fresh Parmesan cheese, (this is about 1.5 ounces)
  • 2 cups chopped onion, (I prefer purple onions)
  • 8 nan garlic cloves, minced (I just used the canned minced)
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 teaspoon minced rosemary or 1/4 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 3 nan (15.5 oz) cans chickpeas, rinsed and drained
  • 1 nan (14.5 oz) can diced tomatoes, undrained
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Instructions

Heat 2 Tb of oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion brown until clear, then add the garlic and stir until both are brown. Don't burn the garlic! Stir in 4 c water, 1 ts rosemary, ¾ ts sea salt, ¼ pepper, 3 cans chickpeas (drained) and 1 can tomatoes (not drained). Bring all of it to a boil, reduce heat, and then simmer for 20 minutes.
Take soup off heat. Puree the soup. I love my hand held Cuisinart mixer (which I got for my birthday from Sus! Thank you) which purees everything to a creamy texture in 3 minutes! You can also blend it by cupping half the soup in a blender, blending it, and then saving it in another bowl until it is all pureed.
Finally, bring all of the soup back into the pot. Stir in balsamic vinegar and bring to a boil. Place soup in bowls and garnish each bowl with 1 tablespoon Parmesan cheese. YUM!
