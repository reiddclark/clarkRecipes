Chicken Enchiladas

Description

No description

Tags

Yield

12 Servings

Ingredients

  • 1 cup salsa
  • 1 teaspoon cumin, ground
  • 1 can (8 oz) tomato sauce
  • 1 can (15 oz) tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 12 nan corn tortilla
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 cup onion, chopped
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1 1/2 pounds chicken breast
  • 1 1/3 cups monterey jack cheese, shredded

Instructions

Cut chicken into bite-size pieces. Spray skillet with nonstick spray and cook chicken over medium-high heat until it is no longer pink, about 5 minutes. Place in large bowl and set aside. When cooked, add 1 cup salsa, 1/2 cup sour cream and 1 cup cheese. Spray skillet again and add onion. Saute over medium-high heat for 2-3 minutes, adding chicken broth as needed to keep the onions from browning. Add garlic, cumin and chili powder, then tomatoes, tomato sauce and the rest of the chicken broth. Bring to boil, then lower heat, cover pan and cook about 15 minutes. Preheat oven to 350 degrees. Spray 9-by-13 inch pan with nonstick spray and set aside. Dip tortillas, one at a time, in tomato mixture and place about 1/3 cup chicken filling in each one, rolling up and placing seam side down in prepared pan. Add remaining sour cream to sauce and pour over enchiladas in pan. Sprinkle remaining 1/3 cup cheese over top, and bake in preheated oven for 30 minutes, or until heated through.





Per Serving (excluding unknown items): 209 Calories; 9g Fat (38.9% calories from fat); 15g Protein; 17g Carbohydrate; 3g Dietary Fiber; 41mg Cholesterol; 427mg Sodium. Exchanges: 1 Grain(Starch); 2 Lean Meat; 1 Vegetable; 1/2 Fat; 0 Other Carbohydrates.
